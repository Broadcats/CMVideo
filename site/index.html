Why no YouTube download here?

YouTube’s Terms of Service forbid third-party servers from downloading their content, and YouTube actively blocks cloud-hosted scrapers at the network level. Running a public YouTube downloader on a free host would be both a ToS violation and a constant cat-and-mouse game.

So instead, when you paste a YouTube URL here we embed the official YouTube player and auto-mute the swears in your browser. No video bytes ever touch our server. Embedding is explicitly allowed by YouTube’s ToS.

Every other source still works as a normal download here — Vimeo, TikTok, Reddit, X, Twitch, SoundCloud, Bandcamp and ~1,800 other sites that yt-dlp supports.

And YouTube downloads work perfectly in the full desktop app — because it runs on your machine, on your IP, as a personal-use tool. That’s the legally clean path and the one that doesn’t depend on whatever YouTube’s anti-bot system did this week.

Drag-drop or URL

Replace flagged words with crisp silence, a classic 1 kHz censor beep, or a retro robotic TTS overlay that turns a 7-hour stream into something genuinely watchable.

Three steps

  1. 1

    Drop a file or paste a URL

    MP4, MOV, MP3, WAV, OGG locally - or any YouTube / 1,800+ site URL supported by yt-dlp.

  2. 2

    Pick what to remove

    Swears, slurs, or both. Choose Silence, Beep, or Fun TTS. Optionally save a full transcript.

  3. 3

    Hit Censor

    CMVideo transcribes, finds every match (including phonetic / leet-speak variants), and writes the cleaned file next to the original.
